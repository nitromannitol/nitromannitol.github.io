I am currently at the University of Pennsylvania as an Assistant Professor of Mathematics (formal start date July 2026).

Before coming to UPenn, I was an NSF Postdoctoral Fellow / Courant Instructor at the Courant Institute with Scott Armstrong. I spent the first year of my NSF postdoc at Cornell University with Lionel Levine. I received my PhD at the University of Chicago under the supervision of Charles K. Smart in June 2022. Previously, I was an undergraduate student at Stanford University.

My research is in probability theory, with a focus on applying ideas from elliptic PDE to problems in statistical physics. I am interested in homogenization, which describes the large-scale behavior of random/periodic systems. I also study fluctuation-driven phenomena that emerge at small scales. Topics I have worked on include diffusions in random environments, Liouville quantum gravity, discrete unique continuation, and the Abelian sandpile.

I co-organize the Penn/Temple probability seminar.
